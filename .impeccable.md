# .impeccable.md

## Design Context

### Users
Couples aged 20–30 in South Korea who want to privately record and recall their date spots and travel memories without social friction. They're busy professionals who need to capture moments (photos) with minimal friction—capturing a place in ≤30 seconds—and retrieve memories reliably months later via map or timeline. The primary user is often one partner (the "record keeper") who initiates logging, while both partners browse and reminisce together.

### Brand Personality
**Intimate, lightweight, and reliable.** The tone is conversational but respectful of privacy; it emphasizes simplicity and trust rather than social validation. Avoid gamification, notifications, or "engagement tactics"—instead, position the app as a quiet keeper of shared memories that respects the couple's autonomy and downtime.

### Aesthetic Direction
Clean, minimal, and photo-centric design with generous whitespace and typography-led hierarchy. Photography should feel warm and personal (slightly desaturated earth tones as accents). Reference apps like Apple Photos, Evernote, and Polarsteps for calm, uncluttered interfaces. Maps should be subtle backgrounds, not visual dominance; cards (place name + photo + rating) should be the primary content unit.

### Design Principles
1. **Speed over perfection**: Every action defaults to sensible auto-fill (EXIF GPS, reverse-geocoded place names); manual correction is secondary, not required.
2. **Photo-first, text-optional**: Design surfaces photos prominently and treat all text fields (one-liners, categories) as enhancers, not blockers.
3. **Predictable retrieval**: Consistent sorting (visit date, descending), search, and map views ensure the user can find a place in under 1 second after 6 months.
4. **Private by default**: No sharing buttons, no social feeds, no comparative metrics; the interface should reflect that this is an archive for two people, not a performance.
